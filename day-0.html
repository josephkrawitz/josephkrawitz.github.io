Plane ride, continued. I watched The Last Samurai and ate airplane food a few times: hot meal (dinner), blueberry loaf thing (breakfast), hot meal (lunch). There were cameras on the plane at the top of the tail and underneath, and you could watch them in real-time on the screen. That was cool and different. Landed in Tokyo to find a gargantuan immigration line. Once we were finally through, we got our checked bag. Then‚Ä¶ we had to go through customs. Meaning we had to wait in another terrible line. We used the same barcode as at immigration and I have no idea why they were different lines. A devastating delay. Once we were finally through the lines, which took us on a tour of a giant room stuffed with travelers, we went through a door into the terminal proper.

Our first order of business was to take care of our checked bag. It was empty, brought along so that packing for the return would be easy. I did extensive research for this trip, and that included notes on the layout of the airport. I knew exactly where to go for the luggage forwarding service. We managed to get it set up with a clerk who didn't speak English, so hopefully it will all be correct.

Why is it called Bic Camera if there are no electronics? I was interested in checking it out, but it was a general store. Past that, we reached the correct kiosks and got our Suica cards set up. Suica is one of the brands of IC cards, which are like Metro Cards, Charlie Cards, etc. in that they can be used for trains and buses. They are also commonly accepted in lieu of cash or a credit card at places like 7/11 and some vending machines. It would be silly to visit Japan without one. Anyway, we got them. Then Jacob immediately forgot what a metro card is for, and said we should get our train tickets. To be fair, it was a very long plane ride. We were not firing on all cylinders.

We took a busy train to a busy train station. We each picked out an ekiben (train station bento box) and managed to purchase Shinkansen (bullet train) tickets to Kyoto, and then actually find the place to board an unreserved seat car on a train that was, in fact, going to Kyoto. It was totally full, of course, but after the first stop (ours would be the 3rd or 4th), we got seats. Not next to each other, but on the same car, so that was still good. I managed to get our carry-ons on the overhead shelf near our seats and kept track that we took everything with us when we left.

At last, Kyoto station. We stretched our legs by walking from there to find our hotel, instead of taking the local train or bus to get as close to the hotel as possible. I was happy to see our first Sakura tree on that walk. We did the self check in, got our things ready for the morning, and tried to sleep. Some of us with fewer interruptions than others. That's ok, I'm accommodating by nature. I was happy he slept well. I'm playing host.
