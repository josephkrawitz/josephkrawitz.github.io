Alas, the dreaded day has come. We were up and out early, headed to the airport at around 7:30. We took the express Asakusa train line. At the airport, things started off quite slowly. We had slightly less time to explore in advance of our 11am flight than anticipated. The walk from security to our terminal opened up at the center of a semi-circle, with gates along the outside. We split up on a reconnaissance mission, looking for a place to get lunch to bring onto the plane and/or a decent souvenir shop to spend the last of our yen. There was shockingly little on either side and for either purpose here! Still, there was a spot that had some decent offerings: tea, chopsticks, that sort of thing, though no t-shirts. Truly, the stuff that you take for granted will be found in excess at a major international airport was nowhere to be found. Finally, a couple of small snacks at the little airport 7/11 took the last of our coins.

I don't really have much to say about the plane ride back! It was nighttime in the US, so the goal was to get as much sleep as possible. Jacob wanted to note that he had Japan Airlines' exclusive "Skytime" beverage, made of peach and grape juice. We crossed the international date line and according to local time, we landed at about the same time on the same day that we departed. 13 hour time difference, 13 hour flight.

Baggage claim is never fun, but we managed. We took an Uber from JFK to the ShopRite of Pelham Parkway, where we were picked up by Caitlin for the last leg of the journey home. Well, the journey to my home. Jacob and I opened up our luggage, which had our belongings mixed about between us, and sorted our things out.

Small souvenirs: The deer, monkey, and fox omikuji from Nara is here, as well as a lucky cat ceramic omikuji. On the far sides are two Ghibli characters made of plastic, the Totoro having hole inside that could store an omikuji slip. In the center is a "Snoopy Scenery" blind box I got for Caitlin.

Jacob then boarded the Grandma Express to go to our parents' house, where he was staying before driving himself back to his apartment.
