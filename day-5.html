Early Morning: Onsens & Gora Departure

I set my alarm for 5am because 5:15 is when the onsen open. I have the private bath on our balcony, but of course, I am still curious about the 'real' ones. I'm in my samue and take my bath basket when I set out to explore the hotel in the soft light of dawn.

I thought I found them, but my first stop was actually the 3 private onsen which do not require reservations or an additional fee, and that I hadn't known existed in advance (or I wouldn't have cared so much about having our own in our room). But the lanterns outside each door were all on, so it seemed like they were already taken by the day's first occupants. Ah well. Moving on.

I continued and found the two communal baths. I was told before the trip that if there were any kanji worth learning, it was the ones for male and female. Good news, I remembered them! I went in the men's changing room, not interested in committing the most stereotypical snafu ever.

Yep, it's a locker room. There's a monitor playing an animation on loop with an English narrator explaining the onsen etiquette for tourists. Fortunately, I knew pretty much all of it. The video was kinda funny though. The guy was like "oh god, no don't do that, that's gross!" about things like washing yourself off standing instead of sitting on the stool. (You thoroughly wash your entire body in the shower part first, so that the water in the hot spring bath itself stays clean, both ritually and because it doesn't get drained and refilled nearly as often as you would want)

I kinda wanted to see the pretty pool of water and surrounding greenery, but I only made it down to my undies before changing my mind. I didn't expect to get the whole thing to myself, but there were enough old Japanese men who didn't seem in the mood to make friends with a foreigner first thing in the morning in the bathhouse. Nobody did anything to put me off, but I felt like I might be a cause of discomfort even though I was pretty confident I knew the proper etiquette. So I figured I'd let them have their thing and I'd go back to the private ones.

An older Japanese couple was just leaving as I was arriving. The lamp didn't immediately go out to show the door was unlocked, which is how it is meant to work, so it's good I saw them myself. Well, I went in, took a look, and decided against it again. It's a little pretty, but also tightly fenced in. Overall, not worth all the extra hassle compared to my private bath at my room. So I did that again instead. Jacob woke up a bit later, at around 6.

Fast forward a bit and Jacob and I are both awake, we are packed, and we are heading out. It would've been cool to spend more time taking advantage of this nice relaxing space, but it just didn't make sense to return here before the designated checkout time. We headed out to Gora Station and it was‚Ä¶ shockingly empty. It's not like it's 5am anymore, it's 7:30. Apparently, the ropeway isn't open that early, so there's nobody coming in or out. We figured out the correct platform to stand at for our train and practically had it to ourselves.

Afternoon: Hakone-Yumoto and Enter: Tokyo

We boarded the bus and made our final big decision for the Fuji-Hakone region: the bus will go the entire way to the Shinkansen station. The bus let us out at street level, just in front of the station and below its main platform. Unexpectedly, right there by the base of the stairs to go up into the station, is a theme shop for one of the few anime that I actually have an interest in: Neon Genesis Evangelion. It's a weird psychological sci-fi from the 90s that takes place in the future - 2015 - and is about high schoolers piloting giant robots to fight colossal alien monsters. It is also kind of about the psychology of deeply emotionally damaged individuals, but the creator has also complained that people dig into it too much and that it isn't really that deep..? Dunno. Either way, it had a fairly big cultural impact here, so it is one that you see hints of randomly throughout Japan. Even so, a store right here? Well, we have to check it out, right?

If we had successfully gone down the ropeway yesterday, this wouldn't have been as much of a surprise.
